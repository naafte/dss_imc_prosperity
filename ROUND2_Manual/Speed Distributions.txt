Distribution 6: Bimodal distribution (Gemini)

Instead of a smooth natural curve, expect a highly unnatural, psychological shape. It will likely look like a bimodal distribution (two distinct peaks of different sizes) heavily scarred by unnatural vertical spikes.

The "Oblivious" Peak (0% – 10%): A small, stubborn peak at the very bottom. These are players who saw the massive numbers that Research and Scale output on paper and decided to go all-in there, completely ignoring the Speed multiplier.

The "Dead Zone" (10% – 25%): Very few bids. It’s too expensive to be a throwaway bid, but too weak to actually win any meaningful rank against the try-hards.

The "War Zone" Bulge (35% – 55%): This is where the vast majority of the data will sit. It will be skewed to the left (meaning a thick, dense build-up that drops off sharply as you approach 60%). Players know they must bid high here to survive, but they are terrified of starving their Base PnL.

The Schelling Point Spikes: Piercing through that main bulge, you will see massive, dense vertical spikes at clean numbers. Human psychology dictates that you will see a disproportionate number of bids at exactly 33.3% (the even split), 40%, 50% (the half-measure), and perhaps 55%.

Because equal investments share the same rank, landing exactly on one of those Schelling points (like 50%) is highly volatile. You might end up tied with 15% of the player base, causing a massive rank collision.

Distribution 7: Noisy (Claude)

Expect a behaviorally-noisy distribution: a cluster at v=0 from players who either reason it through or just focus on R+S, a big mass between v=20–40 from players who "hedge," and a tail out to v=60+ from speed-dominant players. 
The full arms race scenario is possible in a competitive tournament culture. 
The low arms race scenario requires a level of coordination that's unrealistic without communication.
